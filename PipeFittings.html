Key Differences

  • Concentric: Both pipes centered on same axis, vertical lines
  • Eccentric: One side flat, prevents air pockets, horizontal lines
  • Concentric used in gas/vapor lines (vertical orientation)
  • Eccentric used in liquid lines to avoid cavitation

Flange Types Comparison

  • Weld Neck: High pressure/temperature, best strength & fatigue resistance
  • Slip-On: Easy installation, lower cost, moderate pressure
  • Blind: Used to close pipe ends or vessel openings
  • Socket Weld: Small bore high-pressure applications
  • Threaded: No welding required, easy maintenance
  • Lap Joint: Used with stub ends, allows bolt hole alignment

  • Standards: API 6D, ASME B16.34, BS 5351, IS 14846

Valve Types & Applications

  • Ball Valve: Quick shut-off, low torque, oil/gas/water systems
  • Gate Valve: Full bore, minimal pressure drop, on/off service
  • Check Valve: Prevents backflow, protects equipment
  • Globe Valve: Throttling service, flow regulation, steam lines
  • Butterfly Valve: Large diameter, lightweight, quick operation
